Our Commitment to Ethical Publishing

IJDST is committed to ensuring that only original and high-quality research is published in our journal. We strictly follow the guidelines proposed by the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE). All researchers, editors, and reviewers are expected to uphold these standards throughout the publication process.

Guidelines for Researchers / Authors

Researchers wishing to publish in IJDST must adhere to the following principles:

01

Originality: Research content must be original. Plagiarism must not exceed 20%.

02

No Duplicate Submission: The work must not be published or submitted elsewhere.

03

Quality: Content must be free from spelling and grammatical errors.

04

Ethical Conduct: Research must be conducted ethically and comply with relevant legislation.

05

Honest Reporting: Results must be presented clearly without fabrication or falsification.

06

Reproducibility: Methods must be described clearly so findings can be confirmed.

07

Accuracy: Content must be accurate, consistent, complete and unbiased.

08

No Harmful Content: Must not be misleading, hateful, defamatory, abusive, or vulgar.

09

Proper Attribution: Cite all data, designs, methods and ideas appropriately.

10

Copyright Compliance: Reproduce material only with permission and acknowledgment.

11

Author Roles: Disclose contribution of each author in multi-author papers.

12

Funding Disclosure: Declare all sources of funding and material support.

For full details, refer to COPE's International Standards for Authors.

Editor Duties

  • Make fair, unbiased decisions independent of commercial considerations.
  • Maintain confidentiality of submitted manuscripts.
  • Ensure thorough peer review process for every submission.
  • Avoid conflicts of interest in editorial decisions.
  • Investigate misconduct claims and issue corrections when needed.

Reviewer Duties

  • Provide objective, constructive, and timely review reports.
  • Treat manuscripts as confidential documents.
  • Declare any conflict of interest before accepting a review.
  • Alert editors to any ethical concerns or suspected plagiarism.
  • Decline review if the topic is outside their area of expertise.

Plagiarism Policy

All submissions are screened using plagiarism detection software. Manuscripts with similarity above the acceptable threshold will be rejected without review.

Consequences of Violations

Although these guidelines are to be followed by researchers and authors, if a violation is discovered during review or before/after publication, the entire research article will be removed/unpublished from the journal. Repeat offenders may be barred from future submissions and reported to their affiliated institutions.

Report an Ethical Concern

For any concerns, complaints, or suggestions related to publication ethics at IJDST, please reach out to our editorial team.
